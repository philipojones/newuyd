Our Story

Empowering Youth, Transforming Communities

United Youth Developers (UYD) was founded with a simple yet powerful vision: to empower young people across Uganda to become agents of positive change in their communities. What started as a grassroots initiative has grown into a comprehensive youth development organization.

2018

UYD was founded by passionate young leaders committed to addressing youth unemployment and empowerment challenges in Uganda.

2020

Launched our first major programs in education, agribusiness, and leadership development, reaching over 1,000 youth across 15 communities.

2023

Expanded to environmental conservation and tourism programs, planted 50,000+ trees, and established partnerships with international organizations.

Today

Serving 50+ communities with over 5,000 youth empowered through our six core programs across multiple districts in Uganda.

Our Mission

To empower young people with knowledge, skills, and opportunities that enable them to drive sustainable development and positive transformation in their communities across Uganda and beyond.

Our Vision

A world where every young person has the power, resources, and platform to create meaningful change, build prosperous communities, and contribute to a sustainable future for all.

Core Values

Empowerment

We believe in the inherent potential of every young person and work to unlock their capabilities.

Community

We foster strong, inclusive communities where young people support and uplift one another.

Sustainability

We promote environmentally conscious practices and sustainable development in all our initiatives.

Integrity

We operate with transparency, honesty, and accountability in all our relationships and activities.

Our Team

Meet Our Leadership Team

Passionate young leaders dedicated to empowering youth and transforming communities across Uganda through innovative programs and sustainable development initiatives.

Dr. James Okello

Executive Director

Dr. James Okello

Executive Director

Dr. Okello founded UYD in 2018 after seeing the challenges faced by unemployed youth in rural Uganda. With a PhD in Community Development from Makerere University, he has over 15 years of experience in youth empowerment and has led initiatives that have transformed over 50 communities across Uganda.

Calvin Gerald

Programs Director

Calvin Gerald

Programs Director

Calvin oversees UYD's six core programs including education, agribusiness, and environmental conservation. A former secondary school teacher from Kampala, she has developed curriculum that has reached over 5,000 youth across Uganda. Her innovative approach integrates traditional knowledge with modern skills training.

Philipo Jonas

Education Coordinator

Philipo Jonas

Education Coordinator

Philipo leads UYD's education initiatives, focusing on vocational training and digital literacy. Having grown up in a rural village, he understands the educational barriers faced by Ugandan youth. He has partnered with local schools to develop after-school programs that have improved academic performance by 40%.

Grace Auma

Youth Development Officer

Grace Auma

Youth Development Officer

Grace specializes in life skills and leadership development for young women. She has mentored over 200 girls through UYD's programs, helping them overcome cultural barriers and pursue higher education. Her work focuses on building confidence and providing practical skills for economic independence.

Joseph Ssemwanga

Agriculture Specialist

Joseph Ssemwanga

Agriculture Specialist

Joseph leads UYD's agribusiness programs, teaching sustainable farming techniques to rural youth. A farmer himself from Masaka district, he has helped establish over 20 community gardens and trained 500+ youth in modern agricultural practices, increasing crop yields by an average of 60%.

Simon Peter

Digital Innovation Lead

Simon Peter

Digital Innovation Lead

Simon brings technology to rural communities through UYD's digital literacy programs. He has developed mobile learning apps used by over 2,000 youth and established computer labs in 15 underserved schools. His passion is bridging the digital divide in Uganda's rural areas.

Christine Namukasa

Community Outreach Manager

Christine Namukasa

Community Outreach Manager

Christine coordinates UYD's community partnerships and volunteer programs. She has built relationships with over 30 local organizations and mobilized 200+ volunteers. Her work has expanded UYD's reach to remote communities that were previously inaccessible to youth development services.

Dr. Sarah Nabwire

Environmental Coordinator

Dr. Sarah Nabwire

Environmental Coordinator

Dr. Nabwire leads UYD's environmental conservation efforts, having planted over 50,000 trees across Uganda. With a background in environmental science from the University of Nairobi, she educates youth on climate change adaptation and sustainable practices that benefit both communities and the environment.
